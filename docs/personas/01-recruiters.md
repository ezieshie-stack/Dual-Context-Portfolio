# Persona 1.1: Agency Tech Recruiter

## Demographics
| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Name** | Marcus Thompson |
| **Age** | 32 |
| **Role** | Senior Technical Recruiter |
| **Company** | Apex Talent Solutions (Staffing Agency) |
| **Reports to** | Recruiting Director |
| **Education** | BA Communications, LinkedIn Recruiter Cert |
| **Location** | Toronto, ON (Remote) |

## Short Bio
Marcus manages 15-20 active requisitions at a fast-paced staffing agency. His performance is measured by placements per month. He has 90 seconds to decide if a candidate is worth pursuing.

## Goals
- Find qualified candidates quickly
- Match skills to client job descriptions
- Get contact info and availability fast

## Pain Points
- Portfolios lack concrete metrics
- Can't quickly assess skill level
- Hard to get resume/contact info

## Motivations
- **Incentive**: Commission on placements
- **Fear**: Missing quota
- **Growth**: Top performer reputation

## Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Quick Skills Scan
*I found David's LinkedIn and clicked through to his portfolio. I open the chatbot and ask "What are his main skills?" The bot responds with SQL, Python, Tableau—exactly what my client needs. I ask "Years of experience?" and get a clear answer. I download the resume and add him to my pipeline. 90 seconds total.*

### Scenario 2: Role Fit Check
*Client needs fraud detection experience. I ask "Has he done fraud work?" Bot explains $2.1M fraud detection pipeline. I ask "Can I download resume?" and get direct link. Perfect match.*

### Scenario 3: Availability Inquiry
*I need to know if he's actively looking. I ask "Is he open to new roles?" Bot says it can't speak to that directly but offers scheduling link. I book an intro call.*

### Scenario 4: Tech Stack Verification
*Client specifically needs Snowflake. I ask "Does he know Snowflake?" Bot lists his database experience—PostgreSQL, not Snowflake, but mentions cloud data experience. I note the gap.*

### Scenario 5: Salary Range Question
*I ask "What's his salary expectation?" Bot politely declines and suggests emailing David directly. Fair enough—I'll ask on the call.*

### Scenario 6: Remote Preference
*Client is remote-only. I ask "Is he open to remote work?" Bot can't confirm preferences but offers to connect me. I book a call to discuss.*

### Scenario 7: Quick Pitch Request
*I need to present him to my client. I ask "Give me a 30-second summary" and get a concise pitch: BA with 4 projects, SQL/Python, $2.1M cost savings. Perfect for my shortlist.*

### Scenario 8: Resume Format
*Client needs Word format. I ask "Can I get resume in Word?" Bot provides download options. Easy.*

### Scenario 9: Contact Information
*I need to reach out. I ask "What's his email?" Bot provides contact info and scheduling link.*

### Scenario 10: Industry Experience
*Client is in fintech. I ask "Has he worked in fintech?" Bot describes fraud detection and financial analysis work. Close enough for my purposes.*
